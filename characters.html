Carol Peletier (The Walking Dead)

Carol Peletier is an important character in "The Walking Dead," known for her remarkable transformation from a submissive housewife to a formidable survivor. Initially portrayed as timid and emotionally battered by her abusive husband, Ed, Carol's evolution begins with the loss of her daughter, Sophie, which propels her into a journey of self-discovery and empowerment. As the series progresses, she sheds her passive persona and becomes resourceful, learning vital skills such as hunting and self-defense. Carol's character arc is marked by her resilience and determination, which not only help her endure the harsh realities of a post-apocalyptic world but also empower her to protect others, establishing her as a central figure in the show.

Katniss Everdeen (The Hunger Games)

Katniss Everdeen grew up in District twelve and shared similar traits with the people there. She had dark hair, olive skin and grey eyes. Many people from her district such as herself were tough, independent, resourceful, fiery and skilled with a bow as they needed to hunt a lot. Katniss Everdeen is the main provider for her family and is fiercely protective of her younger sister, Primrose. Her deep love for Prim drives her to volunteer in her place for the Hunger Games, an act of self-sacrifice that defines her character.

In District 12, starvation is a constant threat, and Katniss and her family struggle to survive. To provide for them, she turns to hunting though illegal is a necessary skill that she masters. Growing up under such harsh conditions has made her tough, unsentimental, and highly practical.
